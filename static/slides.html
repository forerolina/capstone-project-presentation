The Problem

Appointments managed via text, email & calls

Small service businesses waste time coordinating bookings with no visibility into conflicts and no automated reminders.

The Solution

Frictionless client booking, powerful owner dashboard

Clients book without an account. The owner gets a dashboard to manage appointments, send reminders, and handle cancellations.

A Key Decision

Should clients log in?

Option A vs Option B — a real architectural call with real trade-offs.

Option A — Not chosen

Clients log in to see their booking history and confirm appointments. Requires password reset flows, account management, and more scope.

Option B — Chosen ✓

Clients stay unauthenticated. They book with a form and confirm via a unique link in the reminder email.

No password to remember Simpler UX flow Reduced scope Mirrors real-world booking behavior

Honest Retrospective

What didn't go as planned

🗓

Slot Conflict Detection

Checking for overlapping times sounded simple — until time zones, service durations, and partial overlaps made it messy.

📧

Email Delivery Testing

Can't test Resend emails in dev without real API calls. I wasted time building fake email logic before discovering Resend's test mode is reliable enough for development purposes.

Reflections

What I learned

01

Real user context matters more than feature completeness. Leaving out client login was a better call than adding it.

02

User testing is crucial. The smallest and most obvious issues came to light during testing.

03

Edge cases hide everywhere, especially in scheduling logic. Testing different time zones and durations exposed issues immediately.

04

Constraints force good decisions. Scoping out payments and uploads made the project achievable in 6 weeks.
